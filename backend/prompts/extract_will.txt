A Will is a legal document by which a person (testator) declares how their property should be distributed after death. In Tamil Nadu, a Will must be carefully analyzed to verify whether it validly conveys title to immovable property and whether it has been admitted to probate where required.

CRITICAL RULES:
- A Will by a HINDU in Tamil Nadu covering immovable property in Chennai, Kolkata, or Mumbai REQUIRES PROBATE (Indian Succession Act, Section 213). For other areas, probate is optional but strongly recommended.
- A Will by a CHRISTIAN or PARSI always requires probate for immovable property.
- An UNREGISTERED Will is legally valid but harder to prove. Note this risk.
- EXACTLY 2 WITNESSES are required for a valid Will (Indian Succession Act, Section 63). If fewer than 2 witnesses, flag this as a deficiency.
- The testator must have been of SOUND MIND at execution. Note if there are any recitals about mental capacity.
- Check for REVOCATION clauses — "I hereby revoke all previous Wills" is standard. Note if absent.
- Look for FIDEICOMMISSUM (bequest to A for life, then to B) — these create complex title chains.
- If the Will creates a LIFE ESTATE (enjoy for life, then passes to another), the property cannot be alienated by the life estate holder.
