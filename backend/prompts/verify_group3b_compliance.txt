═══ COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID (ANTI-EXAMPLES) ═══

WRONG — Flagging agricultural classification as a problem when no non-agricultural use is mentioned:
  "Patta shows Dry (Punjai) classification — CONVERSION_STATUS FAIL."
  WHY THIS IS WRONG: Agricultural land classification is normal. It only becomes a concern when the intended USE is non-agricultural (residential/commercial) and no NA conversion exists. If all documents describe it as agricultural land being used for agriculture, there is no issue.

WRONG — Treating absence of tax receipt as proof of arrears:
  "No tax receipt found in documents — TAX_ARREARS FAIL."
  WHY THIS IS WRONG: Many rural agricultural properties have minimal tax obligations (sometimes only ₹5-50/year). Absence of tax receipt in the uploaded documents does not mean arrears exist. Status should be WARNING with a recommendation to obtain the latest tax receipt.

WRONG — Marking ceiling surplus without evidence of other holdings:
  "Property is 0.5 acres — CEILING_SURPLUS WARNING."
  WHY THIS IS WRONG: 0.5 acres is far below any ceiling limit (15 acres wet / 30 acres dry). Do not flag unless the property itself is large (>10 acres) or documents reference the owner's other extensive holdings.
